Hey, I'm Matt Ravenhall. Currently, a PhD student within the PathogenSeq group at the LSHTM.
In my free time I tackle data science projects, communicate science and listen to all the podcasts.

Computational Biologist
My research interests primarily concern genetic diversity within a global malaria context. Currently I focus on the impact of a range of genetic variation within human and Plasmodium (the causal parasite family for malaria) populations. These variants can take multiple forms but I have a particular interest in the distribution and selection of those ranging from small single base variants to larger structural variants (deletions, duplications etc.). A selection of my published work can be found in the publications page with specific deep dives also appearing in the blog section.

Data Scientist
In my spare time, I like to hone my data science skills through a range of side projects. I hold a particular interest in applying 'from scratch' machine learning methods such as neural networks and natural language processing. Recently I completed an internship within the financial data science industry, during which I built automated anomaly detection and rule inference engines. Discussion of some of these projects will take place within the blog section, and source code can usually be found on my github.

Science Communicator
I also hold a keen interest in science communication. Previously, I led the creation of a student science magazine Spark during my undergraduate, and have dabbled in podcast production (such as with the Mendal's Finches) podcast. My writing has appeared in NewScientist and Scientific American and I previously served as a student representative on the ABSW board. Links to some of my communication work can be found via the project page.
