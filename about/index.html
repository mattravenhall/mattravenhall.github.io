Hi I'm Matt Ravenhall, currently a Life Science Specialist at Lingumatics. I also recently completed a PhD in host-pathogen malaria genomics at the LSHTM. In my free time I tackle data science projects, communicate science and listen to all the podcasts.

Bioinformatics

My research interests primarily concerned genetic diversity within a global malaria context, specifically the impact of a range of genetic variation within human and Plasmodium (the causal parasite family for malaria) populations. These variants can take multiple forms but I applied a specific focus upon the distribution and selection of those ranging from small single base variants to larger structural variants (deletions, duplications etc.). A selection of my published work can be found in the publications page with specific deep dives also appearing in the blog section.

Data Science

I like to hone my data science skills through a variety of side projects and hold a particular interest in applying 'from scratch' machine learning methods such as neural networks and natural language processing. During my PhD I completed a financial data science internship, within which I built automated anomaly detection and rule inference engines. Discussion of some of these projects will take place within the blog section, and source code can usually be found on my github.

Science Communication

Every now and then I take to science communication. This has meant leading the creation of a student science magazine Spark during my undergraduate, a dabbling in podcast production (such as with the Mendal's Finches) podcast, and some of my writing appearing in NewScientist and Scientific American. Links to some of my communication work can be found via the project page.
