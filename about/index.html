Hello, I'm Matt Ravenhall. Currently, a PhD student within the PathogenSeq group at the LSHTM.
In my free time I tackle data science projects, communicate science and listen to all the podcasts.

Computational Biologist
My research interests primarily concern genetic diversity within a global malaria context. Currently my work focuses upon the impact of a range of genetic variation within human and Plasmodium (the causal parasite for malaria) populations. These genetic variations can take multiple forms but I have a particular interest in the distribution and selection of those ranging from small single base variants to larger structural variants (deletions, duplications etc.). A selection of my published work can be found in the publications page with specific deep dives also appearing in the blog section.

Data Scientist
In my spare time, I like to hone my data science skills through a range of side projects. I hold a particular interest in applying 'from scratch' machine learning methods such as neural networks or natural language processing. Discussion of some of these projects will take place within the blog section, and source code can usually be found on my github.

Science Communicator
When not writing code, I can often be found communicating science. This interest began during my undergraduate, where I led the creation of a studence science magazine Spark, and has since extended into podcast production such as Mendal's Finches. My writing has previously appeared in NewScientist and Scientific American and I previously served as a student representative on the ABSW board. Links to some of my communication work can be found via the project page.
