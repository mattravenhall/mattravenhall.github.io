Overview

Approximately half the world's population are threatened by malaria, with nearly half a million people dying from the disease each year.[1] The disease is caused by infection, via mosquito bite, with parasites of the Plasmodium genus, with five species involved in human disease: falciparum, vivax, malariae, knowlesi and ovale. Through my research, I aim to identify and understand the impact of genetic variation upon Plasmodium virulence and drug resistance.
